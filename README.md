### Task 1: Business Problem Statement

#### Background

The company recently introduced a new onboarding and activation campaign aimed at improving the conversion of new users into paying subscribers and increasing early product engagement. To evaluate its effectiveness, users were randomly assigned to either a **Control Group** (existing onboarding experience) or a **Treatment Group** (new onboarding campaign).

#### Business Decision

The key decision is whether the new onboarding and activation campaign should be rolled out to all users or whether the current onboarding experience should be retained.

#### Stakeholders Impacted

This decision will impact:

* New and prospective users
* Product Management Team
* Marketing Team
* Customer Success Team
* Senior Leadership and Revenue Teams

#### Success Metrics

The treatment should demonstrate improvement in the following primary business metrics:

1. **User Conversion Rate** – Percentage of users who become paying subscribers.
2. **User Activation Rate** – Percentage of users completing key onboarding milestones.
3. **Early Engagement Metrics** – Product usage during the initial user journey (e.g., sessions, feature adoption, activity rate).

#### Risks and Guardrail Metrics

While improving conversion and engagement, the experiment must not negatively affect important business health metrics such as:

* User churn rate
* Subscription cancellation rate
* Drop-off during onboarding
* Customer support tickets/complaints
* User satisfaction metrics (if available)

These metrics act as guardrails to ensure that gains in conversion are not achieved at the expense of user experience or long-term retention.

#### Evidence Required

Before making a recommendation, the experiment results must provide evidence that:

* The Treatment Group performs better than the Control Group on the primary success metrics.
* Any observed improvement is statistically and practically significant.
* Guardrail metrics remain stable or improve.
* The expected business impact justifies a full-scale rollout.

#### Problem Statement

**The objective of this analysis is to determine whether the new onboarding and activation campaign delivers a meaningful improvement in user conversion and early engagement compared to the existing onboarding experience, while maintaining acceptable performance on key guardrail metrics, thereby supporting a data-driven recommendation on whether the treatment should be launched to all users.**
